# Why This Exists

We're a small business.

When Mythos dropped on April 7, we looked for guidance. CISA? Nothing for small businesses. SANS? Nothing. Our trade association? Nothing. Our cyber insurance carrier? Silence.

The twelve companies that got Mythos access have a hundred million dollars and a coordinated defense plan. We had a computer and a weekend.

So we built this.

We used AI to research. We called our contacts. We read every source we could find — Anthropic's red team report, Forrester's analysis, Bruce Schneier's commentary, vendor blogs, government statements, independent research. We organized it into something a small business owner could actually use.

It's not perfect. It's not written by a pentest firm. It's written by people who actually need this stuff — and who figured if we needed it, a lot of other people did too.

---

## What We Believe

**Security guidance shouldn't be gatekept behind enterprise budgets.**

The same AI that can find zero-days in OpenBSD can find them in your accounting software. The difference is that OpenBSD has Glasswing partners scanning it right now. Your accounting software doesn't.

That gap — between what's being protected and what's being left exposed — is why this repo exists.

## What We're Asking

**Star it.** So others can find it.

**Fork it.** Adapt it for your industry, your stack, your language.

**Fix our mistakes.** We're not infallible. If you find an error, open a PR.

**Add your expertise.** We need stack guides, industry addenda, translations, and tool recommendations from people who know those domains.

**Share it.** With every small business you know. Every IT person. Every accountant, lawyer, doctor, teacher, and shop owner who uses a computer.
