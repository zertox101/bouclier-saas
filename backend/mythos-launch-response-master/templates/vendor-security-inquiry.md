# Vendor Security Inquiry Template

Copy, customize, and send to your critical vendors.

---

**Subject:** Security Posture Inquiry — [Your Company Name]

Dear [Vendor Name] Security Team,

In light of recent developments in AI-driven vulnerability discovery — specifically Anthropic's Claude Mythos disclosure and Project Glasswing — we are conducting a security review of our vendor relationships.

We would appreciate responses to the following:

1. What is your patch cadence for critical security vulnerabilities? (Specifically: what is your target time from CVE disclosure to deployed patch?)
2. Do you have a vulnerability disclosure program or bug bounty?
3. Are you participating in or benefiting from Project Glasswing or similar AI-assisted vulnerability discovery programs?
4. What is your incident response and customer notification timeline if a breach affects our data?
5. Do you maintain SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, or equivalent security certification?
6. When was your last penetration test, and were all critical and high findings remediated?
7. How is our data encrypted at rest and in transit?
8. What is your backup and disaster recovery posture for our data?

We understand this may take time to compile. A response within 30 days would be appreciated. If you have a standard security questionnaire or trust center, a link to that would also be helpful.

Thank you for your partnership in maintaining our shared security posture.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Your Title]
[Your Company]

---

## When to Send This

- **Immediately** to any vendor that stores or processes your sensitive data (financial records, PII, health data, client information)
- **Within 30 days** to any vendor with network access to your systems
- **Within 90 days** to all remaining vendors

## Red Flags in Responses

- Vendor doesn't have a vulnerability disclosure program
- Patch cadence is measured in months, not days
- No SOC 2, ISO 27001, or equivalent certification
- Last pentest was more than 18 months ago
- Vague answers about encryption ("yes, we use encryption" without specifying what kind)
- No incident response timeline committed
- No response at all within 30 days
