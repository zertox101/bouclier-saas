# Project Glasswing Dossier

**Date:** April 11, 2026
**Source confidence:** HIGH (primary sources: Anthropic official, major tech press, government reporting)

## 1. Overview

**Project Glasswing** is Anthropic's restricted defensive cybersecurity initiative, announced simultaneously with the Claude Mythos Preview disclosure in early April 2026. It is not a product. It is not a service available for purchase. It is a **controlled deployment of the most capable vulnerability-discovery AI ever built**, limited to pre-approved partners working on defensive security applications.

**Official URL:** https://www.anthropic.com/glasswing

**Stated mission:** *"Enabling defenders to begin securing the most important systems before models with similar capabilities become broadly available."*

## 5. What Glasswing Does NOT Do

This section matters most for organizations not at the table:

| Gap | Impact |
|-----|--------|
| **No SMB access** | Small businesses cannot use Mythos to scan their systems |
| **No proactive notification** | If Mythos finds a bug in software you run, you won't be told directly |
| **No patch acceleration** | Patches still flow through normal vendor channels at normal speed |
| **No threat intel sharing** | Findings are not broadly shared with the security community |
| **No small business guidance** | No tailored recommendations for SMBs |
| **No insurance guidance** | No cyber insurance impact assessment |
| **No regulatory framework** | Government response is meetings, not mandates |

### The Core Truth

**Glasswing protects the floor (major OS, browsers, cloud). It does not protect your walls, doors, or windows.**

Your niche business software, custom applications, specific configurations, and third-party integrations are NOT being scanned by anyone with Mythos access — unless you arrange it yourself.

## 6. Criticism and Governance Concerns

| Concern | Source |
|---------|--------|
| **Accountability gap** | Private company unilaterally controlling access to critical security capabilities | 
| **Marketing motivation** | Timing coincides with anticipated Anthropic IPO later in 2026 |
| **False scarcity** | AISLE research shows open models can replicate many findings |
| **Defender asymmetry** | Big tech partners get months of lead time; SMBs get nothing |
| **International gap** | No international government or non-US representation in founding partners |

### Fair Assessment

Glasswing is better than nothing and probably the right structural approach. Responsible disclosure, partner vetting, and coordinated patching are sound practices. The criticism is primarily about the gap between what's being protected (big tech, critical infrastructure) and what's being left exposed (everyone else).
